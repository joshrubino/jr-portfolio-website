OutSound - UI Design

Overview

The goal of OutSound is to help users discover new music. They can do that through their own searches and app suggestions that have been curated for the user based on their listening history. OR they can learn about a new artist or song from other users’ profiles and by sharing with friends from the app.

View full case study

Sample Mockups

The following screens are a sampling of the first iteration delivered for the OutSound app.

The 'Splash' screen leads into the 'Create Account' screen, which provides many different options for a user to begin their journey.

To accentuate the curated suggestions for an individual user, the 'Home' screen utilizes the bright images of artist press photos, whereas the Explore screen is a bit more broad and uses a bluish overlay so as to keep everything equal and allow the user to discover new music without too much outside influence.

Keeping the elements somewhat large on the 'Play' and 'Share' screens was important. The user can quickly get the result from their chosen action.

Whether searching or going through one’s saved songs on the 'Search' and 'Liked' screens, the user can quickly play or share a song or artist from there without having to do so from a different screen.

Each user can create a Profile and have a list of songs that they suggest. Other users can see this information and quickly learn about a new song by playing or sharing.
